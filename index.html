Ansh Shah

I am currently a research fellow at the Robotics Research Center (RRC), IIIT Hyderabad, under the guidance of Prof. Madhava Krishna. Over the past two years, I have delved into 3D vision and robot learning, gaining hands-on experience in areas such as learning-based control, differentiable motion planning, 3D metric depth prediction from images, view-invariant object-level association, and multi-agent SLAM. My current focus is on 3D multitask language-conditioned imitation learning, where I am actively engaged in advancing the field.

I enjoy identifying problems and solving them, particularly in the field of robotics and machine learning. My research interests focus on the trio of action, perception, and cognition, where I aim to build machines that can perceive and understand the real world, recognize the physical consequences of their actions, and explore novel behaviors to achieve complex goals. I am especially driven by the challenge of creating generalist agents that can adapt to new tasks without task-specific training data. I believe it is going to take a lot of breakthroughs in the fields of Continual Learning, Cognition, Robot Learning and Skill learning from videos to reach this goal. I believe that high level cognition can be easliy mimicked (Moravec's paradox) shown by research on Knowledge Graph based LLM planners and many more such ideas. We are limited by data on the low level skill learning front where the only abundant modality we can rely on is videos which is sub-optimial to understand the 3D world.

Major Projects

MetricGold: Repurposing Diffusion-Based Image Generators for Metric Depth

Ansh Shah, K. Madhava Krishna
[PDF]

This paper explores an approach for monocular metric depth estimation that utilizes generative diffusion models and a log-scaled metric depth representation, achieving sharper and more accurate metric depth predictions across diverse scenes through synthetic data training.
